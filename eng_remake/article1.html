According to the Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations (FAO), some of the key challenges in the farming industry are: rising food demand driven by population growth and simultaneous constraints on land, water, and soil quality.

According to (FAO, 2025), the population is expected to reach 9.7 billion by the year 2050. Supporting this many people would require an increase in production of approximately 50% more food by that time while competing with other industries for land and water resources.

Soil quality and health is also a major issue. The increase in production of food to support population growth has necessitated the increased use of fertilizer and other biohazardous materials. In addition to pesticides, climate change and other issues, this has created poor nutrient use efficiency (NUE) - a critical measure of the amount of crop yield unit of nutrient in the soil. As of 2023, the world NUE rate is 54.3%. This means over 40% of nutrients are wasted and represents an area ripe for improvement.
